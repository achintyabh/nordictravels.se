History of NordicTravels

NordicTravels was originally a Family-Run Skiing business in the 1930's, aiming to compete with the Austrian Skiing companies. They set up stands in all the big cities; Moscow, London, Paris, New York, Barcelona, Berlin, Bonn, Montreal, you name it! It was based in Uppsala, Sweden, and was recently purchased by the Nordic Co-Operation as an official tour representative of the Nordic Council. We get 85% of our funding directly from the European Union, and we only get about 15% of our funding through sales.

Our Danish Tours

Copenhagen (2 days)

Explore Nyhavn, and go on the SANDEMAN Walking Tours. Tips (DKK 90) are covered by the cost of your package. Eat and Rest at Novotel Christiania.

Aarhus (2 days)

Visit the Tivoli!, as Dansk Natives call it, (funfair). If you want a more peaceful environment, have a nature walk through the old town. Lunch is Dansk Pølse. Sleep at the Holiday Inn Aarhus.

Billund/LEGO TRIP (2 days)

Does that name ring a bell? It was the birthplace of a childhood toy, LEGO! Visit the founder of LEGO's former workshop, and have 1 day at the Legoland Billund Resort. Try the LEGO fries, it is awesome. Accomodation at the Legoland Hotel, Lunch is Dansk Pølse.

Greenland (14 days)

WARNING: EXTREME TEMPERATURES UP TO -30°C, DO NOT GO IF PREGNANT, OLD, WHEELCHAIR USER. YOU HAVE TO SIGN A WAIVER. IF UNDER 16, PARENTS HAVE TO SIGN A WAIVER FOR THE CHILD/MINOR.

Explore the snowy beauty of Nuuk. This tour is close to being sold out. The Inuit are not Indo-European like most Norse, they migrated from the Siberian Plains, and their language is mutually unintelligible to Norwegian, Swedish or Danish.
